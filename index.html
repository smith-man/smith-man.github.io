Recent Projects

EX350 Development

One of the biggest projects I worked on at Webb Electronics was the development of a brand new PCB (printable computer board) called the EX350. This board was designed to increase the number of elevator phones that can be connected to the main lobby station, another product of Webb Electronics. I had the opportunity to contribute to the early design process such as the design specifications, requirements, and GPIO pin plan. I was tasked with early firmware debugging and proofs of concept for many functions we wanted the board to have (ex. SPI Screen, CAN support, RS485 support). I was also able to gain some experience with linux kernel development, specifically in compiling device trees.


Thermal Vaccine Shipper

Recently, I worked with a student team at the University of British Columbia with the goal of redesigning the thermal shipping units that Pfizer is using to distribute the COVID-19 vaccines. The challenge was that the vaccines needed to be transported at -60 to -80 degrees Celsius. We generated a design, made a virtual prototype of the design, planned the electrical layout, and wrote the code required to run the electronic components.


Entryphone Auto-Update System

Another project that I was able to contribute to at Webb Electronics was developing an auto-update system for one of our products so that the software could be updated remotely while they were in the field. With this project, I learned a lot about both front-end and back-end web development. I quickly learned how to write bash scripts as it was an integral part of the auto-update system. Overall, it was a beneficial experience not only because of the skills I learned, but also because it taught me a great deal about software development in a professional, team based environment.


Big Data Analyis: Kidney Cancer RNA Sequences

In my third year at UBC, I was a member of a group in one of my classes that was tasked with the analysis of kidney tumour RNA sequence data from over 500 patients with the goal of identifying subtypes of cancer. We used clustering to find correlation in gene expression and we were able to identify three distinct subtypes from the data set. It was a benefical learning experience that has led me to be far more comfortable with big data analysis.
